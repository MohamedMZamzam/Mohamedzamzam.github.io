Mohamed Zamzam, MSc., PhD

Expertise:

Plant Development, Molecular Biology, Functional Genomics, Bioinformatics

Current positions:

Lecturer of genetics at the Faculty of Agriculture, Al-Azhar University, Cairo Egypt

Biography:

I received a BSc in genetics from Faculty of Agriculture, Al-Azhar University, Cairo in 2008 and then joined the Agricultural Genetic Engineering Research Institute (AGERI), Giza, Egypt, as a research assistant. In 2012, I was appointed as a teaching assistant of genetics at Al-Azhar University. In 2014, I received MSc. in genetics from Faculty of Agriculture, Cairo University. I was promoted to assistant lecturer of genetics at Al-Azhar University in 2017. I obtained PhD in 2023 from the Department of Microbiology and Cell Biology, Faculty of Science, Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. During MSc. I worked on Sorghum bicolor where my research focused on developing improved tissue culture systems for plant regeneration and transformation and for the development of abiotic stress tolerant sorghum. I did my PhD under the supervision of Prof. Usha Vijayraghavan on rice functional genomics and floral development. In April 2024 I was appointed as lecturer of genetics at the Faculty of Agriculture, Al-Azhar University. My current research combines multidisciplinary approaches of molecular biology, microscopy and bioinformatics to understand the fundamental processes that underpin plant development.
